About Me

I am a MSc student and NLP researcher at the Macau University of Science and Technology (MUST), supervised by Qinlin Zhao. My current research interest is latent space reasoning. Previously, I was an NLP researcher supervised by Hui Ye, mainly focused on text classification and graph representation of medical records.

At the beginning of my bachelor’s time, I just happened to join a research group without being aware of what I needed to research. As time went by, I realized that most existence could be, or should be expressed into language, making text itself actually become relatively global “information” instead of local “signals” limited into specific sectors. Therefore, if we need to construct a comprehensive cognition of this world and our minds, along with connecting most features inside, the cognition of language would be a road must follow. That was why I selected NLP at that time.

However, sequential texts limit information and features into a relatively fixed structure, making them hard to be analyzed and applied. Therefore, I attempted to represent text information into graph structure to relationship features subsequently. Similarly, since explicit Chain-of-Thought (CoT) also limits representation and reasoning into sequential tokens, I drop myself into latent space reasoning, and hope to have partners to investigate this new found land.

"I have my duty to give my answer"
